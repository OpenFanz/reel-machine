# Looks Library — the LENS presets (block 2 of every PROMPT)

The lens is HALF the look. Pick ONE preset per clip and paste its language into the PROMPT (block 2 of the
package-spec order). Match it to the reference reel — if the reference is a GoPro porthole, a normal selfie
lens will feel flat/wrong no matter how good the rest is. Combine any look with any shape.

---

## 1 · GOPRO POV ("porthole") — the viral street-interview / walk-up look ⭐
**When:** POV duets, street encounters, prank/reveal reels. The other person holds the cam.
```
…shot on an ULTRA-WIDE FISHEYE action-cam lens (GoPro-style): STRONG barrel distortion across the whole
image, framed by a DARK ROUNDED VIGNETTE — thick curved black corners eating deep into the frame, almost a
circular porthole look. Straight lines (power lines, poles, road markings) visibly BOW outward; the subject
bulges slightly toward the center of the lens. The camera is held CLOSE and slightly BELOW her eye level,
looking up at her — very intimate; she fills the frame from the hips up, her face near the center where the
lens is sharpest…
```
Pair with: the holder's single sleeve/forearm entering ONE bottom corner (POV shapes).
**NEGATIVE add-ons:** flat rectangular frame, no vignette, no fisheye, straight power lines, telephoto
compression, the subject small in frame.

## 2 · PHONE FRONT-CAM SELFIE — the classic UGC look
**When:** solo monologues, selfie duets, CTA talking-heads. She holds the phone.
```
…filmed on a phone FRONT camera at arm's length: mild wide-angle selfie distortion (face slightly rounded at
selfie distance), natural phone exposure, mild grain, her face sharp while the background stays slightly
soft. NO vignette, normal full rectangular frame…
```
**NEGATIVE add-ons:** fisheye porthole, dark rounded corners, DSLR shallow depth of field, beauty-filter skin.

## 3 · DV CAMCORDER (retro Y2K) — the "old tape" aesthetic
**When:** nostalgic day-in-my-life, AYi-style daily vlogs, artsy character reels.
```
…shot like a late-90s DV camcorder tape: slightly soft focus, muted colors with a warm magenta cast, mild
analog noise, gentle motion smear on movement, slight interlace shimmer, handheld amateur framing with
imperfect horizon…
```
Date-stamp / REC overlay: in the EDIT, never generated.
**NEGATIVE add-ons:** crisp digital sharpness, modern HDR colors, clean noise-free image.

## 4 · WEBCAM / LAPTOP — the "caught on stream" look
**When:** desk reels, reaction-style skits, "my viewers asked…" formats.
```
…framed like a laptop WEBCAM: fixed camera slightly BELOW eye level on the desk, she sits close to the lens,
slightly compressed webcam colors, mild sensor noise in the shadows, flat even room lighting from the screen
glow, static framing (the camera never moves)…
```
**NEGATIVE add-ons:** handheld wobble, camera movement, cinematic lighting.

## 5 · CCTV / HIGH-ANGLE — the "security cam caught this" hook
**When:** viral "caught on camera" formats, no-dialogue action beats.
```
…framed like a ceiling SECURITY camera: high corner angle looking down, very wide lens with mild fisheye,
desaturated cool tones, slight noise, static mount (no handheld motion), the scene plays out below…
```
Timestamp overlay: in the EDIT.
**NEGATIVE add-ons:** eye-level framing, warm grading, camera following the subject.

## 6 · THIRD-PERSON PHONE (friend films her) — rear-cam realism
**When:** get-ready/outfit checks, walk-and-talk with a friend behind the camera.
```
…filmed by a friend on a phone REAR camera from a few steps away: normal focal length (no fisheye, no selfie
distortion), handheld micro-shake and small reframes, natural phone HDR exposure, she does NOT hold any
phone — both hands free…
```
**NEGATIVE add-ons:** selfie arm, fisheye, vignette, gimbal smoothness.

---

## Matching a reference reel's look (from the teardown/frames)
Check the frames for: **corner darkness** (vignette → GoPro), **line bowing** (fisheye strength), **camera
height** (below/above eye level), **who holds it** (arm in frame?), **color character** (golden-hour warm /
DV magenta / CCTV cool), **motion** (handheld wobble vs static). Name ALL of these explicitly in the PROMPT —
what you don't name, the model averages away.
